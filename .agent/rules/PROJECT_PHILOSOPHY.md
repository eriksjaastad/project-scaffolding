# Project Philosophy

purpose: Actionable principles that guide project decisions
scope: What kind of projects we build and how we treat them

## Project Identity

rule: we build experiments (data collection, pattern recognition, learning), not products
rule: projects exist because they are interesting, not because they are profitable
rule: every project operates at two levels — domain patterns and meta patterns (building patterns)
rule: the meta patterns compound across all projects — the scaffolding is the real product

## Building

rule: let projects be experiments — data tells you what works
rule: 30-60 day minimum before judging results (especially data collection projects)
rule: do not confuse "fun to build" with "needs to scale" — both valid, different treatment

## Maintenance

rule: consolidate on the 3rd duplicate — first two teach you the pattern
rule: tests are for fragile parts — parsing, grading, data integrity
rule: documentation goes stale — archive aggressively (12mo sessions, 3mo misc)
rule: safety systems are scars — you learn what needs protection by breaking it first

## Data

rule: collect before deciding — gut instincts need validation
rule: let the data change your mind — be willing to kill features after testing
rule: measure what matters — not just "did it run?" but "did it help?"
